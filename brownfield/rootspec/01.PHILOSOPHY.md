# Level 1: Foundational Philosophy

**Product:** RootWeather
**Version:** 7.3.7
**Status:** Baseline

---

## Competitors

- **Weather.com** — Feature-heavy portal with aggressive advertising and cluttered UI
- **AccuWeather** — Dense data presentation aimed at power users and professionals
- **Dark Sky (acquired)** — Beloved for hyper-local precision; discontinued as standalone
- **Weather Underground** — Community-driven data with complex, dated interface

## Table Stakes

- Current conditions (temperature, humidity, wind, UV)
- Multi-day forecast (at least 7 days)
- Hourly forecast for the day ahead
- Location search by city name
- Persistent saved locations

## Pain Points

- **Information overload** — Most weather apps bury the signal in data noise
- **No multi-location awareness** — Switching between cities is cumbersome; no at-a-glance comparison
- **Unit rigidity** — Apps force a single unit system without easy switching
- **Irrelevant alerts** — Push notifications crying wolf; warnings disconnected from current conditions

## Influences

- **Dashboard products** — At-a-glance summaries that respect the user's time
- **Clean iOS apps** — Restraint in visual design; only show what matters now
- **Open-source data philosophy** — Free, no-key weather data (Open-Meteo) as a principle, not a workaround

---

## Mission

Deliver weather clarity — not weather complexity. RootWeather exists to answer the question "what's the weather like?" without burying the answer under ads, dark patterns, or feature bloat.

## Design Pillars

### 1. Instant Clarity
Users get the answer they came for in seconds, without hunting.

**User perspective:** "I opened the app and immediately knew what to wear."

**Examples:**
- Current conditions visible above the fold with no scroll
- Alerts surface automatically based on actual data thresholds

### 2. Effortless Multi-Location
Users who care about multiple places — travelers, remote workers, people with family elsewhere — feel equally at home checking any city.

**User perspective:** "I can glance at all my cities at once, then drill in."

**Examples:**
- Favorites bar for fast switching in single-city view
- Dashboard view shows all saved locations with live data
- Side-by-side comparison available for up to 3 cities

### 3. My Settings, Always
The app remembers how users prefer to see data — units, time format, default city — without ever asking again.

**User perspective:** "It just shows °F because that's what I set."

**Examples:**
- Temperature unit (°C / °F) persists across sessions
- Wind unit (km/h / mph) persists across sessions
- Default city loads automatically on next visit

## Inviolable Principles

- Never show ads or tracking; weather data should be a utility, not a product
- Never require account creation or authentication
- Always surface condition-based alerts from the data itself, not from a third-party push system
- Never lose user settings — preferences and favorites are always persisted locally
