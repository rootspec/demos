# Level 1: Foundational Philosophy

## Competitors

- **Weather.com** — Feature-heavy, ad-laden, slow to load
- **AccuWeather** — Precise but cluttered UI with paywalled features
- **Dark Sky (acquired)** — Beloved for simplicity; no longer available as standalone
- **Weather Underground** — Data-rich but overwhelming for casual users
- **Willy Weather** — Clean but limited comparison and location tools

## Table Stakes

- Current conditions (temperature, wind, humidity, UV)
- Short-term hourly forecast
- Multi-day forecast
- Location search
- Data persistence across sessions

## Pain Points

- **Information overload** — Most weather apps bury the useful signal under noise
- **Friction to get started** — Apps requiring accounts or permissions before showing data
- **Single-location lock-in** — Switching between locations is awkward or requires navigation
- **No comparison** — Can't see two cities side-by-side without opening two tabs
- **Non-configurable units** — Apps assume a unit preference without letting users change it

## Influences

- **Dashboard tools** — Overview-first design that lets users absorb many data points at a glance
- **Progressive disclosure** — Simple on first visit, more available when you want it
- **Local-first software** — Preferences saved in the browser, no account required

## Mission

Make weather information immediate, honest, and useful — without registration, clutter, or paywalls. RootWeather delivers what you need to make a real-world decision, then gets out of your way.

## Design Pillars

### 1. Instant Clarity
Users get the answer they came for in under three seconds, without scanning past noise.

**User perspective:** "I opened this and knew immediately what to wear."

**Examples:**
- Current conditions appear as the primary element after any city search
- Weather state communicated via emoji + temperature at a glance
- No login walls, no modal overlays, no ads between the user and data

### 2. Effortless Comparison
Comparing cities should feel natural — not like a workaround.

**User perspective:** "I could see both cities at once and just… decide."

**Examples:**
- Dashboard view surfaces all saved locations simultaneously
- Comparison mode places full forecasts side-by-side
- Entering compare mode is a single action from the dashboard

### 3. Personal Without an Account
Settings and saved locations persist without creating an account.

**User perspective:** "It remembers my preferences without asking me to sign up."

**Examples:**
- Favorites, units, default city, and time format all saved to localStorage
- App loads with the default city already shown on return visits
- No onboarding friction

### 4. Honest Data
Weather data is presented accurately without manipulation or upselling.

**User perspective:** "I trust what it shows me."

**Examples:**
- Data sourced from Open-Meteo, a free, open, no-key API
- Conditions and alerts derived algorithmically from real values, not curated
- No sponsored forecasts or promoted "premium" accuracy

## Inviolable Principles

- Never require login or account creation to access any feature
- Never show advertisements or paywalled content
- Never obscure primary weather data behind promotional content
- Always surface weather alerts when conditions warrant them
- Preferences saved locally — no backend, no tracking
