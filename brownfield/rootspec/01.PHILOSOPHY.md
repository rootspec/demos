# Level 1: Foundational Philosophy

## Competitors

- **Weather.com** — Feature-rich but ad-heavy, slow to load, pushes premium content
- **AccuWeather** — Detailed forecasts buried under cluttered UI and upsells
- **Apple Weather** — Clean but locked to Apple ecosystem, limited customization
- **Google Weather** — Minimal and fast but no multi-location dashboard or saved preferences

## Table Stakes

- City search with autocomplete
- Current conditions (temperature, humidity, wind, UV)
- Hourly and multi-day forecast
- Temperature unit toggle (Celsius/Fahrenheit)

## Pain Points

- **Ad overload** — Most weather sites prioritize ad revenue over readability
- **Account walls** — Saving locations or preferences requires sign-up
- **Information sprawl** — Radar maps, pollen counts, and news articles bury the basics
- **Platform lock-in** — Best experiences limited to native apps on specific platforms

## Influences

- **Minimal dashboard apps** — Clean cards, generous whitespace, data-forward design
- **Browser start pages** — Ambient info you glance at, not study
- **Widget culture** — Small, focused, always-there weather displays on phones

## Mission

Give people instant, ad-free weather for the places they care about — in a browser, with zero setup.

## Design Pillars

### 1. Effortless Clarity
Weather information is immediately understandable at a glance, never buried or overwhelming.

**User perspective:** "I opened it, I saw the weather, I moved on."

**Examples:**
- Large temperature display with emoji condition indicators
- Color-coded alert badges that surface without user action

### 2. Familiar Comfort
Your saved locations and preferences are always there, like a window you look out of every morning.

**User perspective:** "It remembers my cities and my units — it just works for me."

**Examples:**
- Favorites persist across sessions without login
- Default city loads automatically on launch

### 3. Ambient Awareness
Relevant conditions and warnings surface naturally without hunting through data.

**User perspective:** "It told me about the UV and wind without me asking."

**Examples:**
- Condition-based alerts appear automatically above weather details
- Precipitation probability shown inline on forecast cards

## Inviolable Principles

- Never require an account or login for any functionality
- Never show advertisements or sponsored content
- Never paywall features — everything is free and open
- Always respect user preferences without requiring explicit re-entry
