# Level 1: Foundational Philosophy
<!-- L1: WHY & WHAT EXPERIENCE — External references only -->

## Product Vision

RootFeed is a Bluesky-like social feed built to demonstrate how RootSpec transforms a bare SvelteKit scaffold into a complete, interactive experience with minimal human guidance. It is not a real product — it is an honest, playful prototype that shows developers what specification-driven development looks like in practice.

The question it answers: "I already have routes and a framework. How does RootSpec fit in?"

## Problem Statement

Developers evaluating RootSpec need a concrete example that starts where they are — an existing scaffold — rather than a greenfield fantasy. They want to see that a spec methodology works with messy reality: existing files, existing routing, existing assumptions. RootFeed provides that example, transparently.

## Target Audience

Developers considering RootSpec who already have a project with routing and a framework in place. They are skeptical of spec methodologies that only work from scratch.

## Competitive Landscape

Similar demos and social feed clones exist (Twitter/Bluesky clones, Hacker News readers, etc.) but none are purpose-built to showcase a specification methodology applied to a pre-existing scaffold. RootFeed's differentiator is not the product itself — it is the transparency about how the product came to be.

## Table Stakes

For a social feed demo to be credible, it must have:
- A working feed of posts with author attribution and timestamps
- The ability to navigate to profiles and individual posts
- A search surface
- A discovery/explore surface
- Recognizable social interactions (likes, follows)
- Visual polish consistent with a 2026 web standard

## Design Pillars

### Playful Honesty
Every screen feels like an enthusiastic collaborator showing off their work, not a product pretending to be something it isn't.

### Alive Without Being Fake
The mock data and interactions feel like a real conversation happened here — real-ish usernames, plausible opinions, threading that makes sense.

### Effortless Orientation
A developer landing for the first time immediately understands what it is, how it was built, and where to look next.

### Spec Transparency
The connection to RootSpec is visible and easy to trace — links to SEED.md, the spec, and the scaffold commit are first-class navigation.

## Inviolable Principles

- The app must never pretend to be a real social network. The demo framing is always visible.
- Every page must make the RootSpec connection clear — this is a showcase first, a social feed second.
- The scaffold origin must be documented and linkable from the live site.
- State is always client-side only. No persistence between page loads.

## North Star

Six months from now, a developer stumbles on this demo, pokes around for five minutes, sees the meta banner, clicks "View the scaffold commit," reads the SEED.md, and says: "Oh — so RootSpec took this skeleton and turned it into *that*? I want to try this on my project." That moment of recognition — surprise at what specification-driven development produced from so little — is the entire point.
