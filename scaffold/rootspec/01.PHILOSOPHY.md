# Level 1: Foundational Philosophy

**Product:** RootFeed
**Tagline:** A RootSpec scaffold demo
**Version:** 7.2.7

---

## Competitors

- **Bluesky** — Decentralized social network with AT Protocol; open-source but complex infrastructure
- **Mastodon** — Federated microblogging; powerful but fragmented and hard to onboard
- **Twitter/X** — The archetype; feature-rich but chaotic, algorithmic, and increasingly hostile to developers
- **Threads** — Instagram's take; smooth UX but closed ecosystem with no API
- **Cohost** — Dev-friendly, quirky social platform; shut down in 2024

## Table Stakes

- Timeline of posts from people you follow
- User profiles with bio and follower/following counts
- Post detail view with threaded replies
- Search posts by keyword
- Explore/discovery surface with trending content
- Like and repost interactions
- Dark and light theme support

## Pain Points

- **Algorithmic opacity** — Most feeds hide why you see what you see; RootFeed is purely chronological mock data
- **Complexity theater** — Real social networks require auth, federation, real-time infra; RootFeed demonstrates the UX without the backend complexity
- **Demo apps feel fake** — Most demos use lorem ipsum and obvious placeholder data; RootFeed uses realistic content that reads like a real community

## Influences

- **Bluesky's UI** — Clean, accessible, chronological feed design
- **Storybook** — A development tool that is also a demo of itself; RootFeed is a demo of RootSpec that happens to be a social feed
- **Vercel's templates** — High-polish demo projects that developers actually learn from

---

## Mission

RootFeed exists to show developers what RootSpec can do with an existing scaffold. It is a social feed app that is honest about being a demo — every surface carries the framing, and the content celebrates the methodology behind it.

---

## Design Pillars

### 1. Alive Without Pretending

The mock data should feel like a real community — real-ish usernames, real-ish conversations, tech humor and opinions. But the framing never lets visitors forget this is a demo. Both things are true at once.

**User perspective:** "This feels like something real people would use, and I can tell it's demonstrating something."

**Examples:**
- Users have distinct voices and personalities in their posts
- A persistent meta banner explains the scaffold origin story
- The footer surfaces RootSpec version, build date, and attribution

### 2. Transparent by Design

RootFeed never hides what it is. The spec, seed, and scaffold commit are one click away. Rough edges are explained, not hidden.

**User perspective:** "I understand exactly how this was built and what choices were made."

**Examples:**
- Meta banner links to SEED.md, spec files, and scaffold commit
- Footer attributes the RootSpec version prominently
- Interactive features are client-side only — no pretense of persistence

### 3. Playfully Technical

The tone is developer-to-developer: direct, self-aware, with a sense of humor. Not corporate, not cutesy.

**User perspective:** "This was made by someone who actually codes."

**Examples:**
- Post content includes real tech debates, in-jokes, and casual opinions
- UI is clean but not over-designed — functional first
- Interactions feel deliberate, not magical

---

## Inviolable Principles

- Never pretend to be a real social network — every page must feel like a demo that happens to be a social feed
- Never use lorem ipsum or obviously fake content in mock data
- Never hide the RootSpec origin — attribution and transparency are non-negotiable
- Client-side state is not persisted across page loads — do not imply otherwise
- Only routes pre-rendered from mock data exist — do not spec "not found" pages or server-side fallbacks

---

## North Star Experience

A developer evaluating RootSpec lands on RootFeed, scrolls the feed, clicks into a profile, reads a thread. They notice the content feels real but the framing is honest. They click the meta banner and land on the spec. They think: "I could start with a scaffold like this and end up with something like this." That moment of clarity — RootSpec turned an empty skeleton into a complete, tested experience — is the north star.
