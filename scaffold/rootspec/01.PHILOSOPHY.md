# Level 1: Foundational Philosophy

**Product:** RootFeed
**Tagline:** A RootSpec scaffold demo
**Version:** 1.0.0

---

## Competitors

- **Bluesky** — Decentralized social network, AT Protocol-based, growing developer community
- **Mastodon** — Federated microblogging, ActivityPub-powered, fragmented UX across instances
- **Twitter/X** — The incumbent microblogging platform; familiar but increasingly adversarial to users
- **Threads** — Instagram-adjacent social feed; polished but algorithmically opaque

## Table Stakes

- Timeline feed showing posts in reverse-chronological order
- User profiles with bio, follower/following counts, and post history
- Search and discovery of posts and users
- Like, repost, and reply engagement on posts
- Hashtag/tag browsing and filtering

## Pain Points

- **Black-box algorithms** — Most platforms obscure why you see what you see; no transparency
- **Engagement theater** — Metrics are optimized for platform retention, not genuine connection
- **Demo-to-reality gap** — AI-built demos look polished but feel hollow; no authenticity
- **Missing provenance** — You rarely know how or why an app was built the way it was

## Influences

- **Bluesky/AT Protocol** — Clean, chronological, developer-friendly design language
- **RootSpec methodology** — Transparency about the build process as a first-class feature
- **Open-source developer culture** — "Show your work" as a design value
- **Prototype aesthetics** — Honest about being a demo; not pretending to be something it's not

---

## Mission

Demonstrate that a bare SvelteKit scaffold can become a complete, realistic social feed experience through the RootSpec specification process — and make that transformation legible to every developer who visits.

---

## Design Pillars

### 1. Honest Aliveness
The feed feels real — real-ish names, real-ish conversations, genuine interactivity — while being transparently a demo, never pretending to be a production social network.

### 2. Effortless Navigation
Moving through the feed, profiles, posts, and discovery surfaces feels fluid and intuitive — nothing gets in the way of exploring the content.

### 3. Transparent Construction
The spec, seed, and scaffold origins are always one click away — this demo celebrates how it was built, not just what it built.

### 4. Playful Credibility
The content and interactions feel technical and grounded — not lorem ipsum, not whimsical nonsense — but the framing is always a well-made prototype, never a pretend product.

---

## Inviolable Principles

- Never remove or hide the meta banner — transparency about the demo's nature is non-negotiable
- Never pretend the data is real or persists — all state is client-side and reset on reload
- Never build features that require a server — this is a static site deployed to GitHub Pages
- Always link to the actual spec and seed files in the GitHub repository using absolute URLs
- The name "RootFeed" and the RootSpec attribution must appear on every page
