# Level 1: Foundational Philosophy

**RootFeed — A RootSpec Scaffold Demo**

---

## Competitors

- **Bluesky** — Decentralized social feed with AT Protocol, clean UX but real product stakes
- **Twitter/X** — The definitive social feed reference; familiarity, scale, and dysfunction
- **Mastodon** — Open-source, federated, high-friction for newcomers
- **Threads** — Instagram-backed, stripped-down, algorithmically managed
- **GitHub Discussions** — Developer-focused async discourse, not real-time

## Table Stakes

- A scrollable timeline of posts from known authors
- Author identity: handle, display name, avatar
- Post-level engagement counts (likes, reposts)
- Replies and threads (parent/child relationships)
- Navigation to author profiles and individual posts
- Search across post content
- Discovery surface for new users and trending content

## Pain Points

- **Real apps obscure how they work** — Social feeds pretend to be magic; RootFeed makes the construction visible
- **Scaffold tutorials feel sterile** — Blank routes and placeholder text don't show how spec + implementation fit together
- **AI demos hide the scaffold** — Most "built by AI" claims show only the finished product, not the process or starting point
- **Demo projects aren't taken seriously** — RootFeed should feel complete enough to be convincing while clearly signaling it's illustrative

## Influences

- **Bluesky** — Visual rhythm of the feed, author line, engagement actions
- **Linear** — Demo-as-product-quality: even a showcase should feel intentional
- **Observable notebooks** — Transparency as a feature; the construction is part of the content
- **Hacker News** — Functional over decorative; text-forward, trust the content

---

## Mission

Demonstrate what RootSpec's scaffold workflow looks like from the inside — starting with a working skeleton and growing it into a complete, polished experience through spec-driven development.

RootFeed is not a real product. It is a proof-of-concept that answers a real question developers ask: *"I already have a project. How does RootSpec fit in?"*

---

## Design Pillars

### 1. Transparent Construction
The scaffold origin is never hidden — it's celebrated. Visitors should understand that rough edges are the artifact of sparse spec, not sloppy engineering.

**User perspective:** "I can see exactly how this was built, and that makes me trust it more."

**Examples:**
- Meta banner visible on every page explaining the demo's origin
- Links to SEED.md, spec files, and the original scaffold commit
- Attribution footer on every page

### 2. Believable Fiction
The mock data, interactions, and visual design should feel alive — real-ish conversations, authentic-feeling profiles — so the demo conveys what a production app would feel like.

**User perspective:** "This feels like a real app, even though I know it's a demo."

**Examples:**
- Tech-community personas with distinct voices in post content
- Functional like/bookmark/follow toggle states
- Realistic timestamps, engagement counts, and reply threads

### 3. Developer as Audience
Every design decision assumes the visitor is a developer evaluating RootSpec — not a social media user. The product communicates to people who read source code and care about process.

**User perspective:** "I can imagine my codebase looking like this."

**Examples:**
- RootSpec version displayed in the header
- Technical framing in the meta banner and footer
- No dark patterns or engagement mechanics — just demonstration

### 4. Spec-Driven Clarity
The app should reflect what a good spec produces: coherent navigation, consistent visual rhythm, logical data flows — not random decisions bolted together.

**User perspective:** "Everything here feels like it belongs."

**Examples:**
- Consistent layout across all five routes
- Predictable interaction patterns for likes, bookmarks, follow
- Theming (light/dark) that works throughout

---

## Inviolable Principles

- Never pretend to be a real social network — every page should be obviously a demo
- Never hide the scaffold origin — the meta banner must appear on every page
- Never persist state across page loads — all interactivity is client-side only
- Always prioritize developer comprehension over end-user engagement
- No dark patterns, infinite scroll traps, or attention-capture mechanics
- RootSpec version must be visible in the UI at all times

---

## North-Star Experience

A developer discovers RootFeed while evaluating RootSpec. They land on the home feed, see a realistic-looking social timeline, and immediately notice the blue banner explaining the origin. They click through to the spec files, read the SEED.md, look at the scaffold commit, and understand exactly how the project went from empty routes to a working app. They think: *"I could do this with my project."* That thought is the entire goal.
