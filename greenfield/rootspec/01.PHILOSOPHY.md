# Level 1: Foundational Philosophy

**Product:** RootSpec Marketing Site
**Version:** 1.0.0
**Status:** Active

---

## Competitors

- **Conventional README sites** — Documentation-first pages that describe tooling but don't convey methodology
- **Loom/Vercel-style landing pages** — Polished SaaS pitch sites optimized for conversion, not comprehension
- **Spec tools (Linear, Notion, Confluence)** — Collaborative document platforms without philosophical hierarchy
- **AI coding tools (Cursor, Copilot)** — Code generation focused, no spec validation layer

## Table Stakes

- Clear explanation of what RootSpec is and why it exists
- Demonstration of the methodology (interactive elements)
- Path to getting started (GitHub link, install command)
- Author credibility and honest context about the project's state

## Pain Points

- **Spec drift** — Existing tools don't enforce hierarchy; requirements drift silently from original intent
- **Philosophy-implementation gap** — No mechanism for ensuring code satisfies the original "why"
- **AI output unreliability without validation** — AI can generate code but can't verify it satisfies intent
- **Documents nobody reads** — Google Docs specs exist outside the development workflow; they're consulted rarely and abandoned freely
- **Generic landing pages** — Most dev-tool sites look identical (dark backgrounds, gradient blobs, stat counters); they signal "AI-generated" rather than "thoughtfully built"

## Influences

- **Long-form editorial writing** (Stripe, Linear docs, thoughtful Substacks) — typography-first, dense with meaning, earns trust through care
- **The Pragmatic Programmer, A Pattern Language** — Methodology books that changed how developers think
- **Stripe's documentation aesthetic** — Technical rigor with editorial quality
- **Literary magazines** — Readers arrive expecting to think, not to be sold

---

## Mission

Software development has a drift problem: by the time a feature ships, the original intent has been filtered through a chain of interpretation, assumption, and urgency. RootSpec exists to make the specification itself the durable artifact — one that carries human intent, intact, from the first question to the last test. The site must make this argument with clarity and intellectual honesty, using the methodology itself as proof of concept.

---

## Design Pillars

### 1. Earned Trust

Visitors feel they are reading work by someone who thought hard about the problem, not a product page engineered for conversion.

**User perspective:** "This site doesn't feel like it's trying to sell me something. I feel like I'm encountering a real idea."

**Examples:**
- The meta banner is front and center — the site is honest about being a demo, not performatively polished
- Author's voice is present and direct; no corporate hedging or buzzword inflation
- Rough edges acknowledged, not hidden

### 2. Conceptual Clarity

Visitors feel the methodology is genuinely understandable — not dumbed down, and not obscured by jargon.

**User perspective:** "I understand what this is and why it matters. I could explain it to someone else."

**Examples:**
- Interactive hierarchy explorer makes the five-level structure visceral and navigable
- Side-by-side comparison shows the before/after without abstractions
- Short, precise explanations — no padding

### 3. Methodological Credibility

Visitors feel they are looking at a system that would actually work — not an aspirational framework that collapses under real conditions.

**User perspective:** "I can see how this would handle the messy parts of real development. This isn't purely theoretical."

**Examples:**
- Author's Notes are reproduced verbatim — no editorial softening; the argument stands or falls on its own
- Four named skills give the methodology operational concreteness
- The "spec your idea" wizard produces real structural output, not a placeholder simulation

### 4. Aesthetic Integrity

The site's visual quality signals that the methodology produces considered, intentional results.

**User perspective:** "This doesn't look like a default AI-generated site. Someone made design decisions here."

**Examples:**
- Editorial typography (serif body, clean sans UI, monospace for code) not gradient blobs
- Light mode default, restrained palette, generous whitespace
- The diagram looks hand-drawn and purposeful, not like a Visio export

## Inviolable Principles

- **Never misrepresent the demo's nature** — The site was generated from a sparse seed with minimal human intervention. It must say so clearly above the fold. Any impression that this is a "polished marketing site" with full human design work would mislead visitors about what RootSpec actually produces.
- **Author's voice is not editable** — The Author's Notes section must reproduce the verbatim text from the seed. The site's job is to carry that voice through, unaltered.
- **No dark-pattern aesthetics** — The site must not deploy conversion-optimized patterns (urgency cues, stat counters, "trusted by" bars). The aesthetic must support the editorial argument, not undermine it.
- **No external API calls** — Interactive elements run entirely client-side. Visitors must not need to provide data or be tracked to experience the core functionality.
- **Honest version display** — The RootSpec version used to build this site must be visible (read from `.rootspec.json`). The methodology is versioned; the version at build time is a material fact.

---

## North-Star Experience

Six months from now, a developer who was burned by scope drift on a previous project finds the RootSpec site. They read the Author's Notes. They understand immediately that the author has diagnosed the same problem they've lived through. They explore the hierarchy explorer, run through the spec wizard with their current project idea, and walk away with a mental model they can actually use. They click through to the GitHub repo and begin reading the framework. They do not feel they were sold to — they feel they encountered an idea worth taking seriously.
