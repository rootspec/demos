# Level 2: Stable Truths

References: [L1: Foundational Philosophy]

## Design Philosophy

### Truth: Philosophy must be the single source of authority
Every implementation decision traces back to a Design Pillar. If a feature cannot be traced to L1, it does not belong. The hierarchy exists not to slow teams down, but to prevent the accumulation of untraceable decisions.

### Truth: Specification is a validation discipline, not a documentation habit
A spec is not a wiki entry — it is an executable constraint. The test of a good spec is whether you can derive a conforming implementation from it, and whether a nonconforming implementation can be detected. Specs that cannot be validated are decoration.

### Truth: Human philosophy is irreplaceable; implementation is commodity
AI can generate code, tests, and even spec fragments. What AI cannot supply is the human judgment about what a product is *for* and what experience it must create. The framework exists to extract, preserve, and protect that judgment across iterations, team changes, and AI-assisted workflows.

## Architectural Commitments

### Truth: The hierarchy enforces dependency inversion
Lower levels (implementation) must always depend on higher levels (philosophy), never the reverse. This is not a convention — it is a structural rule enforced by the framework. L1 must be writeable without any knowledge of L5.

### Truth: Each concern has exactly one home
Duplication across levels creates drift. If a decision is recorded at L2, it is not re-stated at L3 or L4. References flow downward; definitions stay at their canonical level.

### Truth: Natural language is the primary interface
The spec is written in plain prose and YAML, not in a proprietary DSL or schema-first format. This keeps the methodology accessible to domain experts, PMs, and developers alike — and makes AI assistance productive rather than purely mechanical.

### Truth: We choose validation over documentation
We choose producing a validated, checkable spec over producing comprehensive reference documentation. A shorter spec that can be tested is worth more than a thorough spec that cannot.

### Truth: We choose stability of philosophy over velocity of implementation
We choose keeping L1-L2 stable and changing L4-L5 freely, over moving fast at every level simultaneously. Architectural philosophy should feel boring to update; implementation details should feel safe to change.

### Truth: We choose traceability over flexibility
We choose every feature tracing to a principle, over allowing ad-hoc feature additions that feel locally useful. Features without philosophical roots create long-term incoherence.

## Definition of Success

The RootSpec marketing site succeeds when:
- A developer who has never heard of RootSpec can articulate the methodology's value from a single page visit
- An interactive element (Hierarchy Explorer or Spec Wizard) successfully demonstrates the methodology hands-on
- The site itself serves as a credible proof-of-concept: built with RootSpec, the site visibly reflects the methodology it markets
- Visitors understand that the site was generated from a minimal seed with no design mockups or manual code, and see that as an asset rather than a limitation

## Constraints We Accept

- **No external API calls** — all interactivity runs client-side; no backend, no AI calls from the browser
- **Static deployment** — the site deploys to GitHub Pages at a subpath and must function without a server
- **No design mockups** — the visual design emerges from the spec and implementation, not from pre-designed assets
- **Minimal seed constraint** — the site is explicitly built from a sparse description; rough edges are honest, not careless
