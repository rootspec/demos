## Design Philosophies

### Truth: Show beats tell — the site is the proof
The most persuasive argument for RootSpec is a site that was built with RootSpec. Every section demonstrates the methodology rather than describing it. The meta banner makes this explicit: visitors must understand this is a live demo built from a sparse seed with minimal iteration.

*Supports L1: Proof Over Promise*

### Truth: Honest incompleteness over polished illusion
A demo built from a 100-line seed with no mockups and no iteration will have rough edges. We surface this honestly. Visitors who see the rough edges and understand why trust the methodology more than visitors who see a polished facade and wonder how it was really built.

*Supports L1: Proof Over Promise, Earned Trust*

### Truth: The methodology is the differentiator, not the aesthetics
Visual polish is table stakes. The differentiator is the interactive experience of the methodology itself — the hierarchy explorer, the wizard, the before/after comparison. These must work flawlessly. Visual polish in non-interactive sections is secondary.

*Supports L1: Empowered Curiosity, Clarity Without Ceremony*

### Truth: Developers are skeptical by default — earn attention, don't demand it
Technical audiences read fast, skim faster, and leave immediately if something feels like marketing. Every claim must be specific and verifiable. Every feature must work. Every link must resolve. No lorem ipsum. No placeholder content. No "coming soon."

*Supports L1: Earned Trust, Clarity Without Ceremony*

### Truth: Complexity lives in the tools, not the explanation
The methodology has five levels and a strict hierarchy. This is not simple. But the explanation must feel approachable — layer complexity progressively through the hierarchy explorer rather than front-loading it. First contact should clarify, not intimidate.

*Supports L1: Clarity Without Ceremony, Empowered Curiosity*

## Trade-offs

- We choose **honesty about limitations** over **polished first impressions** — a demo that admits its constraints is more credible than one that hides them
- We choose **interactive proof** over **static documentation** — visitors experience the methodology rather than reading about it
- We choose **client-side only** over **AI-powered personalization** — no external API calls means no fragile dependencies and nothing to explain away when a demo fails
- We choose **developer-to-developer tone** over **broad market appeal** — precision beats reach for this audience
- We choose **progressive complexity** over **complete upfront disclosure** — the hero hooks; the wizard and explorer reward deeper engagement

---

## Definition of Success

The site succeeds when a developer who arrives skeptical leaves with:

1. A clear understanding of what RootSpec is and why it exists
2. A concrete next step (the four skills, the GitHub repo)
3. A lived experience of the methodology through the interactive elements
4. Enough trust in the methodology to try it on a real project

Success is not measured by visual impressiveness. It is measured by whether the methodology lands.

---

## Constraints We Accept

- **No backend, no database** — all interactivity is client-side JavaScript; no data persists between sessions
- **Static site deployment** — hosted on GitHub Pages at a subpath; all asset URLs must resolve under `/demos/greenfield/`
- **No AI integration on the site itself** — the wizard uses structured templates, not language models
- **Sparse seed, minimal iteration** — the site is built from a single [100-line] product description; rough edges are a feature, not a bug
- **Version-pinned framework** — the site reflects the RootSpec version it was built with; the version badge is a first-class element, not a footnote
