# Level 2: Stable Truths

## Truth: Substance over polish

The site's value comes from what it communicates, not how it looks. Visual design serves comprehension — clean typography, clear hierarchy, readable code examples. If forced to choose between a beautiful animation and a clearer explanation, choose the explanation.

**Trade-off:** We choose clarity of message over visual spectacle.

## Truth: Show, don't tell

Every claim about RootSpec must be demonstrated, not just asserted. Saying "specs stay aligned with implementation" means nothing without proof. The interactive features exist to make the methodology tangible — they are not decorative.

**Trade-off:** We choose interactive proof over marketing copy.

## Truth: Self-referential credibility

The site is its own case study. It was built using RootSpec, and that fact is prominently displayed. This constrains what we can do — we can't hide rough edges behind custom polish — but the honesty is worth more than the perfection.

**Trade-off:** We choose transparent demonstration over curated impression.

## Truth: Client-side independence

All interactive features run entirely in the browser. No accounts, no API keys, no backend. Visitors can experience RootSpec without giving anything up. This limits what the wizard can do (no AI-powered spec generation) but removes all friction.

**Trade-off:** We choose zero-friction experience over sophisticated AI features.

## Truth: Developer-native communication

The site speaks to developers as peers, not prospects. Technical accuracy over accessibility to non-technical audiences. Specific over general. Concrete over abstract. The tone is a thoughtful developer explaining something they believe in — not a product page trying to convert.

**Trade-off:** We choose developer trust over broad appeal.

## Success Criteria

- A developer who has never heard of RootSpec can explain what it does after spending [brief duration] on the site
- The interactive hierarchy explorer makes the five-level structure intuitive without requiring the developer to read documentation
- The meta banner creates a "wait, really?" moment that increases credibility
- Visitors who try the spec wizard feel the methodology is approachable, not academic

## Constraints

- No server-side logic — everything static or client-rendered
- No user accounts or data collection
- Must work without JavaScript for core content (interactive features degrade gracefully)
- Single-page or minimal navigation — the site is a single narrative, not a multi-page app
